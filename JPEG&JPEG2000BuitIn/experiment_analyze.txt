Metrics Background:
Mean Squared Error (MSE):
MSE measures the average squared difference between a reference image and its compressed version.
Lower MSE means the reconstructed image is closer to the original.

Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio (PSNR):
PSNR converts MSE into a log-scale score.
Higher PSNR means better quality. 

Structural Similarity Index (SSIM):
SSIM compares images in terms of perceived structure, luminance, and contrast.
Scores range from 0 to 1 (higher is better).

We chose to keep the compression rate for JPEG and JPEG 2000 stays the same to compare metrics better.
